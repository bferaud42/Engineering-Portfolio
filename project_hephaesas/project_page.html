Summary

Project Hephaesas is an AI-assisted workflow designed to speed up hardware design iteration and facilitate design exploration. It works best when a design is specified and specific dimensions are given.

Featuring a multi-agent architecture, the workflow attempts to understand the intent of the prompt and generate a CAD model. It can be exported as a .step file to view in Fusion and SolidWorks.

Hardware Product Design AI-Assisted Workflow CAD Modeling

Problem

  • CAD sits at the center of the iteration loop: “Design” is the point where ideas become manufacturable geometry and where every downstream step (build, testing/analysis, stakeholder review) gets anchored. Because feedback often requires changing dimensions, interfaces, packaging constraints, or manufacturability features, teams repeatedly return to CAD—making CAD a rate-limiting step for iteration speed and time-to-market.
  • Today’s CAD workflows are optimized for experts, not iteration: modern tools assume strong 3D intuition and specialized training, rely on click-heavy GUIs, and create friction for quick conceptual exploration. As design complexity grows, the time cost scales with it—especially when multiple stakeholders need revisions and handoffs.
  • Interoperability and lock-in amplify the cost of change: moving between tools or teams often strips design intent (feature history, parametric relationships), forcing rework and slowing collaboration. The result is that early-stage ideas—where speed matters most—get bottlenecked by CAD overhead, contributing to a pipeline where most concepts never survive long enough to ship.

Approach
